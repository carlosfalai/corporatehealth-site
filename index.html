FAQ

Questions employers ask

Is this legal across Canada?

Yes. The Canada Health Act's extra-billing and user-charge rules apply only to insured medically-necessary services. Everything we sell — workforce screening, executive assessments, IMEs, file reviews, fitness-for-duty — is an employer-commissioned, uninsured, third-party service, billable employer-to-vendor in every province. It's the same lane the national occupational-health firms operate in. If anything ever surfaces a medically-necessary need, that component is routed to the public plan, never billed to you.

What's the difference between the two sides?

Workforce Health is proactive: per-employee programs that improve and measure the health of your people — check-ups, health-risk assessments, executive & preventive care, vaccination, coaching. Medical Risk & Claims is defensive: per-case physician opinions for when a medical claim, disability dispute or accommodation duty lands on HR — IME, file review, fitness-for-duty, return-to-work. Same engine, same physician signature, same privacy wall.

Does the employer ever see medical details?

No. A licensed physician sits between the clinical file and you. On the risk side you receive fitness, limitations, accommodations and return-to-work guidance. On the workforce side you receive de-identified, aggregate population insight. Never the diagnosis, symptoms, medications, or anyone's individual record — mandated by the CMQ Code of ethics and Quebec's Law 25 / PIPEDA.

Who makes the medical decisions?

A board-certified physician — always. Our system organizes the intake, history and records (facts only — it never diagnoses, triages or risk-scores); the physician applies judgment, makes every determination, and signs it. That keeps the service off the regulated medical-device line and puts a real physician's name behind every call.

How does the Bilan de santé work — and what does the fee cover?

For each enrolled employee, by age and sex, we produce the screenings Santé Canada recommends — mammography, colorectal (FIT / colonoscopy), diabetes, depression, cervical, lipids and more — with physician-signed requisitions. The employee does the tests free in the public system (CLSC / hospital). When results return, the physician explains them over Spruce, email or SMS — "all good", or "your mammogram is abnormal, we'll arrange an ultrasound / a surgeon / a repeat in 5 months" — and arranges any work-up through RAMQ. The yearly fee covers the plan, the requisitions, and that physician follow-up; the tests themselves stay publicly covered.

Is this a tax-deductible expense for the company?

Generally yes — employer-paid preventive and occupational health is an eligible business expense, and we provide a receipt. Whether it counts as a taxable benefit to the employee depends on how it's structured (for example, as a Private Health Services Plan); confirm the treatment with your accountant.

What if an employee never logs in or does the tests?

The bilan is billed per enrolled employee for the year — like a benefit premium. It's non-refundable and doesn't carry over to next year, so if someone chooses not to participate, the seat isn't refunded. We help you drive uptake with reminders and the aggregate participation dashboard.

How does payment and document upload work?

For a single case you pay on the site first, then a secure, payment-gated page unlocks where you upload the dossier — records, forms, or the case file. Workforce programs are scoped on a quick call. Everything is handled under signed agreement, encrypted, and processed with Canadian data residency (ca-central-1) for Quebec and enterprise clients.

What about workplace injuries (CNESST / WCB)?

Return-to-work and surveillance arising from an accepted workplace-injury claim are paid by the provincial board at board fee schedules — we exclude that work from employer-billed programs. We serve non-claim absence, group-disability, preventive workforce health, and voluntary or statutory employer-funded services.
